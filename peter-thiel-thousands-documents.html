Peter Thiel Appears in Thousands of Epstein Documents: Lunches, Investments, and a Michelin-Star Buyout

The PayPal co-founder's name appears thousands of times in the DOJ release. Documents reveal years of lunch meetings, a $40 million investment connection, and an exclusive Palo Alto dinner with Musk and Zuckerberg.

Peter Thiel, the billionaire tech investor and PayPal co-founder known for his early support of Donald Trump, appears in thousands of documents in the DOJ's Epstein files release—more than almost any other tech figure.

The documents reveal a relationship between Thiel and Epstein that spanned years, including multiple lunch meetings, email exchanges, and a $40 million investment connection that raises uncomfortable questions about Silicon Valley's ties to the convicted sex offender.

Years of Lunch Meetings

The files contain scheduling entries showing "LUNCH w/ Peter Thiel" on multiple dates from 2014 to 2017. The communications began around 2014 and lasted until around 2019—just months before Epstein was arrested on federal sex trafficking charges.

On April 8, 2016, according to an itinerary in the files, Epstein was scheduled to have lunch with Thiel. A limo service booking confirmation showed a stop at Thiel Capital in San Francisco. Direct email exchanges between the two include a June 2014 policy discussion and multiple invitations to meet.

The $40 Million Question

The financial ties go deeper than lunch. The New York Times reported that Epstein invested $40 million in two funds managed by a venture capital firm co-founded by Thiel in 2015 and 2016.

The investment came years after Epstein's 2008 conviction for soliciting prostitution from a minor—a time when many in finance and tech claimed to have cut ties with him.

The Michelin-Star Dinner

Perhaps the most revealing document is an email about an exclusive dinner in Palo Alto in the summer of 2015. The two-Michelin-star restaurant Baumé was bought out for a private dinner on August 2, 2015.

According to the San Francisco Chronicle's review of the files, the guest list included some of the most powerful names in Silicon Valley. It's unclear which invitees actually attended.

No Island Visit—But Questions Remain

A representative for Thiel has said the venture capitalist never visited Epstein's infamous private island in the Virgin Islands. Authorities have accused Thiel of no wrongdoing.

But the sheer volume of documents—thousands of pages—and the years-long relationship they document raises questions about how much Silicon Valley's elite knew about Epstein's activities, and why they continued to associate with him.

Important Context

Authorities have not accused Peter Thiel of any wrongdoing. A representative confirmed Thiel never visited Epstein's private island. The emails and scheduling documents reveal business and social contact but do not indicate knowledge of Epstein's crimes.
