PHOTO: Mick Jagger Seated Between Jeffrey Epstein and Bill Clinton at Dinner

A never-before-seen photograph released by the DOJ shows the Rolling Stones frontman at a table with the convicted sex offender and former President ‚Äî raising fresh questions about Epstein's celebrity connections.

The Department of Justice's December 2025 release of the Epstein files included thousands of photographs from the convicted sex trafficker's properties and personal collection. Among them: an undated image showing Mick Jagger, the legendary Rolling Stones frontman, seated between Jeffrey Epstein and former President Bill Clinton at what appears to be a dinner gathering.

The photograph is one of several images from the DOJ release showing prominent figures in Epstein's orbit, though the mere presence of someone in these files does not imply wrongdoing.

The same DOJ release included additional photographs of music industry figures. Pop icon Michael Jackson was pictured with Clinton and posing separately with Epstein in front of a painting. Singer Diana Ross also appeared in group photos alongside Clinton and Jackson.

Jagger was seen in at least one other photograph from the release, posing with Clinton and an unknown woman whose face was redacted by the DOJ.

Clinton's Documented Connections

The former president's connections to Epstein have been extensively documented. Flight logs show Clinton took multiple trips on Epstein's private jet, dubbed the "Lolita Express." Clinton has acknowledged the flights but denied any knowledge of Epstein's crimes.

The newly released photos add visual documentation to the known relationship, though they do not indicate Clinton witnessed or participated in any illegal activity.

Response from Mick Jagger's Representatives

Representatives for Mick Jagger did not respond to requests for comment regarding the photographs.

Response from Bill Clinton's Office

A spokesperson for President Clinton stated that the release of old photographs does not change the facts: Clinton "knew nothing about the terrible crimes" committed by Epstein and has "answered questions about his limited contact over the years."

The photograph is among approximately 180,000 images released by the DOJ as part of compliance with the Epstein Files Transparency Act. Researchers and journalists continue to comb through the massive archive, identifying individuals and documenting Epstein's extensive network of connections.
